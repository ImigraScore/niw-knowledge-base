# EB-2 NIW Eligibility Thresholds: A Structural Analysis

> Understanding the dual-layer requirement of the Employment-Based Second Preference (EB-2) National Interest Waiver.

---

## The Dual-Layer Strategy

To succeed in an EB-2 NIW petition, an applicant must satisfy two distinct legal layers. Failure to distinguish between these two is a primary cause of petition denial.

1.  **Threshold Eligibility:** You must qualify for the EB-2 category itself.
2.  **The Waiver:** You must prove that it is in the national interest to waive the labor certification.

---

## Layer 1: Threshold EB-2 Eligibility

You must meet ONE of the following two categories established in 8 CFR §204.5(k):

### A. Advanced Degree Professional
*   **Definition:** A U.S. academic or professional degree above that of a baccalaureate, OR a baccalaureate degree followed by at least five years of progressive experience in the specialty.
*   **Critical Evidence:** Official academic records and letters from current/former employers documenting "progressive" experience.

### B. Exceptional Ability
*   **Definition:** A degree of expertise significantly above that ordinarily encountered in the sciences, arts, or business.
*   **Requirement:** You must satisfy at least **three** of the six criteria (Degree, 10 years experience, License/Certification, Salary, Membership, or Recognition/Achievements).

---

## Layer 2: The National Interest Waiver (Dhanasar)

Satisfying the threshold only proves you belong in the EB-2 category. It does NOT prove you deserve a waiver. Once the threshold is met, the adjudication moves to the **three-prong Dhanasar test**:

1.  The endeavor has **Substantial Merit** and **National Importance**.
2.  The person is **Well Positioned** to advance the endeavor.
3.  On balance, it is **Beneficial to the U.S.** to waive the job offer/labor certification.
