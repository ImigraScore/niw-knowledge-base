# NIW RFE Risk Signals: Why Petitions Fail

> Structured analysis of Request for Evidence (RFE) patterns and common adjudication vulnerabilities in EB-2 National Interest Waiver petitions.

## The Anatomy of an RFE

A Request for Evidence (RFE) is not a denial, but a signal that the evidentiary foundation of the petition failed to meet the *Preponderance of the Evidence* standard for one or more prongs.

Based on analysis of AAO appeals (2020–2025), ImigraScore has mapped the most recurring "Risk Signals."

---

## Top 3 Risk Signals (Post-2025 Updates)

### 1. The "Generalized Endeavor" Signal (Prong 1)
*   **Adjudicator Logic:** "The petitioner's field (e.g., Data Science) is important, but the petitioner failed to show how their *specific project* will impact the U.S. as a whole."
*   **Mitigation:** Narrow the focus to a specific industry, region, or technological gap.

### 2. The "Future Capacity" Signal (Prong 2)
*   **Adjudicator Logic:** "The petitioner has a strong background, but has provided no objective evidence (contracts, investors, specific plans) showing they are *currently* positioned to execute the specific endeavor proposed."
*   **Mitigation:** Shift from "I have a degree" to "I have a roadmap and interest from stakeholders."

### 3. The "Local Impact" Signal (Prong 1/3)
*   **Adjudicator Logic:** "The endeavor only benefits the petitioner's company or a small group of clients, without broader implications for the U.S."
*   **Mitigation:** Demonstrate how the work solves a scarcity problem or creates ripples in a critical field.

## RFE Risk Mapping Table

| Signal | Primary Prong | Adjudication Pattern |
|:--- |:---:|:--- |
| **Vague Endeavor** | Prong 1 | Merging "Field" with "Endeavor" |

| **Lack of Scarcity** | Prong 3 | Failing to show why a Labor Cert would be counterproductive |
| **Circular Merit** | Prong 1 | Arguing merit based on salary alone |
